  1. What are the constitutional rights, if there are, for the inmates and how does it affect them?

    Everybody, without any exception, nationals and foreigners, that are in national territory, enjoy constitutional rights. Fundamental rights that acquire their relevance in the legal world since the Constitution of 1991 in which application is over any other actual normativity in the legal order. The fundamental rights of Colombians are found in Title 2, First Chapter from article 11 to 41 of the Political Constitution of Colombia of 1991, which gives us a total of 31 fundamental rights ( without counting those for connectedness ). Lets remember that not all prison populations are in the quality of condemned, this is, with a sentence on behalf of the judicial operator executor. A big part of the prison populations are simply in syndicated quality, lets say, that over them, lays a measure to ensure appearance at trial of an intramural trial until the criminal process ends. Another part is found only in the condemned sentence in first instance waiting to be resolved in the resource of an appeal or extraordinary cassation and finally those that are deprived of liberty those with a condemned executory sentence, solely for the purpose of serving his sentence. With that being explained, it's possible to establish that independently the condition of the person ( national or foreigner, man, woman or other identity, prisoner or a free person ) the fundamental rights that are plasm in the Political Constitution, are of mandatory application for the State authorities and for particulars. This way, with the right of life in article 11 Constitutional up to the free development of the ibid personality, has to be recognized to the one that has the condition of recluse. Evidently in Colombia, its guaranteed the right of freedom in multiple expressions, however the same, by Constitutional dispositions and in the exercise of the faculty ius puniendi of the State, that right can be limited always and when precepts are verified like the one of legality ( procedures and motives previously established by the law ) and by the order of the competent authority. In that order of ideas, it is sensitive to manifest that the prisoners in Colombia are susceptible of being subjects of observancy to certain constitutional rights or by connection with other rights such as health, conjugal visits, human dignity, free of expression, intimacy between others, that with the action of tutelage, can be an object of verification by any judge of the republic in effects of a vulneration of cessation , grievance of his observancy of his constitutional rights and therefore an order will be given to the competency to protect those fundamental rights.

  3. Can they vote if they have criminal records?

    Whoever is paying an imprisonment cannot vote. Once the person pays for the time of imprisonment, they can vote again. Clarifying the Constitutional Corte through sentence T-324 of 1994 that “ the detainees that have not yet been condemned , are beneficiaries of the presumption of innocence and for political effects, must be considered like citizens with all their rights which deserve a preferred deal because they are in an inferiority situation.

  5. What are the legal obstacles for the inmates to get a job and go back to society?

    In this section it is precisely to mention that the penalty in Colombia does not have a vindictive purpose because of the crime caused. By the contrary, its purpose, as seen on article 4 of Law 599 of 2000 ( Penalty Code) looks for the general prevention, jura retribution, special prevention, social reinsertion and the condemned protection. Although written in the norm, that is far away from reality, more thankfully to the presence of stigmas from society and political ignorance, the reintegration becomes difficult on the execution of the sentence served, like the acts after the sentence. Like jails with a very high crowding index and reiterated ignorance of constitutional rights of the jail population, a lot of times in conditions of special treatments, prevents the inmate from atoneting his conducts and reframe the way he acts in society. From this point of view, if the jail population presents high indices of recidivism, let's say has been through jail once or more times, that indicates that the resocialization is not fulfilling and that there is a population that by a diverse factor, is more susceptible to go back to the center of reclusion. From the previous you can conclude that on a shy way from the reasons mentioned, in Colombia the resocialization in prisons does not put far away for crimes to a considerable number of jailed population and there is an existence of a type of population that by diverse factors ( specially economical and socially) is more susceptible to go back to prison. This indicates that the penal system operates in a selective way over a particular group of people. That then generates in the society, a constant thought of who were condemned, generating a distrust for who was condemned in a jail. Clearly this raises barriers in the obtaining of jobs, housing and intrafamily relations. That is why Colombian law gives the no manifestation of certificates of conduct over an imposed penalty, in the effect of preventing this type of finger pointing and barriers that are object to people that are condemned.
